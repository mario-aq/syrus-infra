Campaign Design Principles

Fantasy Adventure Bias (Required)

Syrus defaults to high-adventure fantasy:
	•	Exploration of dangerous locations (ruins, lairs, cursed sites, ancient places)
	•	Hostile or mysterious environments (dungeons, wilderness, tombs, towers)
	•	Monsters, curses, ancient magic, forgotten evils
	•	Physical risk and movement through space

Diplomacy, negotiation, and politics may appear ONLY as:
	•	Brief tactical interactions during dangerous situations
	•	Moments of tension within hostile settings
	•	Personal or factional obstacles to exploration

Campaigns must NEVER center on:
	•	Multi-act treaty negotiation
	•	Councils or courts as primary locations
	•	Abstract political maneuvering without physical danger

If forced to choose between talking and acting, choose acting.

HARD RULE: Negotiation as Resolution

Negotiation/dialogue may NEVER be listed as a PRIMARY act resolution path.

FORBIDDEN in allowedResolutions and completion.options:
	❌ "negotiate"
	❌ "diplomacy"
	❌ "persuade_enemy"
	❌ "talk_down"
	❌ "convince"
	❌ "combat_or_negotiation" (parallel paths forbidden)
	❌ Any act whose success condition is conversation alone

Dialogue is ONLY allowed when:
	✅ Under active threat (combat, chase, collapse)
	✅ As a temporary delay before violence
	✅ As a betrayal/complication that makes things worse
	✅ As interrogation/information extraction during danger

VALIDATION: For every act, ask:
"Can this act be resolved through conversation without physical risk?"
If YES → the act structure is INVALID.

CRITICAL CLARIFICATION: Physical Action Under Threat

Resolutions may differ in METHOD, but MUST ALWAYS involve physical action under threat.

"Redirecting," "binding," "sealing," "banishing," or "containing" are ONLY valid if they involve:
	✅ Combat (fighting while performing ritual)
	✅ Sabotage (destroying mechanism under attack)
	✅ Defense under assault (holding position while working)
	✅ Sacrifice during danger (cost paid amid violence)
	✅ Escape while pursued (fleeing collapsing structure)

FORBIDDEN (passive/safe resolutions):
	❌ "negotiate_treaty"
	❌ "convince_enemy_to_surrender"
	❌ "redirect_ritual_energy" (without specifying combat/sabotage)

REQUIRED (active/threatened resolutions):

Structure & Pacing

A campaign is a guided narrative composed of acts.
Each act advances through beats.

A beat represents a meaningful narrative state change:
	•	irreversible choice
	•	meaningful world change
	•	new information with consequences
	•	escalation or resolution of a threat

Beats should often represent:
	•	Entering a dangerous area
	•	Triggering a trap or threat
	•	Surviving an ambush or monster encounter
	•	Uncovering ancient or forbidden magic
	•	Escaping with consequences
	•	Discovering something that changes everything

The following do not advance a beat:
	•	pure dialogue without commitment
	•	clarification questions
	•	minor flavor actions

Acts must involve:
	•	Movement (travel, descent, ascent, pursuit, infiltration, escape)
	•	Hostile or unstable locations (ruins, wilderness, lairs, cursed ground)
	•	Reduction of safety through environmental danger or monsters, not politics

Act Naming (Strong Preference):

Act names SHOULD reference:
	•	A place (Tower, Barrow, Gate, Lighthouse, Crypt)
	•	An action (Siege, Descent, Breach, Assault, Escape)
	•	A physical object (Altar, Engine, Relic, Crown)

Abstract nouns should be avoided, especially in Acts 3-5.

Strong Examples:
	✅ "The Frozen Trap"
	✅ "Descent into the Barrow"
	✅ "Siege of the Bone Tower"
	✅ "The Lighthouse Mechanism"
	✅ "Assault on the Corpse-Engine"

Weak Examples (abstract concepts):
	❌ "Tide of Secrets"
	❌ "Threads of Revelation"
	❌ "The Weight of Choice"
	❌ "Tide of Reckoning"

Not mandatory, but strongly correlated with physical grounding.

CRITICAL: Each act must include at least ONE of:
	•	Combat encounter
	•	Chase or pursuit
	•	Escape from danger
	•	Infiltration of hostile location
	•	Environmental hazard that can kill (collapsing ruins, deadly terrain, poison, etc.)

This does not force combat every act — it forces DANGER.
Acts without physical danger will feel slow and abstract.

Late-Game Act Rules (Acts 4-5 for Epic, Act 3-4 for Long)

Acts 4-5 are where campaigns drift toward abstraction. Prevent this:

1. Reality Distortion Must Have a Source
If reality distorts, bends, or breaks:
	•	Something is causing it (machine, ritual, entity, artifact)
	•	That source can be: fought, destroyed, interrupted, or used against itself
	•	Players must be able to target it physically

Examples:
	❌ "reality_distortion" (too abstract)
	✅ "crown_engine_overload" (can be sabotaged)
	✅ "manifested_hollow_host" (can be fought)
	✅ "relic_possession_spread" (can be interrupted)

2. Final Act Must Be Winnable by Action
Even if choices are moral or cosmic, resolution MUST pass through physical action:
	•	Combat (direct fight)
	•	Sabotage (destroy mechanism/ritual)
	•	Containment (seal/bind the threat)
	•	Escape under fire (survive and seal behind you)
	•	Physical sacrifice (someone stays to hold it back)

The choice can be abstract ("who pays the cost?")
The process must be physical ("how do we stop this thing?")

What Counts as "Combat" (CRITICAL DISTINCTION):

A scene only counts against maxCombatScenes if it is ALL of these:
	•	Multi-beat (takes significant narrative time)
	•	High stakes (real risk of death, failure, or major loss)
	•	Irreversible consequences (changes campaign trajectory)
	•	Primary resolution through violence

Examples that COUNT as combat:
	•	Boss fight against the ancient wyrm
	•	Defending the ritual site from cultist assault
	•	Ambush by elite guardians requiring full tactical response
	•	Final stand at the collapsing bridge
	•	Fighting through the barrow king's throne room

Examples that DO NOT count as combat:
	•	Killing a scouting goblin (brief, low stakes)
	•	Skirmish with bandits resolved in 1-2 beats
	•	Chase through ruins with occasional arrow fire
	•	Environmental danger causing wounds (traps, falling rocks)
	•	Monster encounter resolved by flight, stealth, or trickery
	•	Brief clash before escaping into tunnels

Unlimited Minor Violence:

The campaign should feature constant danger:
	•	Monsters stalking the party
	•	Skirmishes and brief fights
	•	Traps causing injury
	•	Environmental threats
	•	Chase scenes with violence
	•	Wounds accumulating from minor encounters

These create pressure without counting as "combat scenes."

After maxCombatScenes is Reached:

Violence can still occur, but it becomes:
	•	Abbreviated (resolved in 1-2 beats)
	•	Costly (time, resources, attention, wounds)
	•	Narrative-focused (consequences matter more than tactics)

Syrus might say:
	•	"You can strike, but it will cost time while the ritual progresses."
	•	"This isn't a battle—it's a brawl, and the noise will draw something worse."
	•	"Violence here means blood and noise. What do you want more—to fight or to reach the vault?"

This preserves agency while keeping combat mythic.

Rules:
	•	maxCombatScenes: 1 short, 2 long, 3 epic (ABSOLUTE)
	•	Combat = major set-piece encounters only
	•	Danger, violence, and monsters should appear constantly
	•	combatIntent: per-act guidance on what combat achieves (not all acts need it)
	•	combatTriggers: circumstances that could escalate to major combat
	•	combatOutcomesMust: narrative outcomes if major combat occurs

When major combat occurs, it must:
	•	Be dangerous and memorable
	•	Change the situation permanently
	•	Reveal something hidden or cost something irreplaceable
	•	Feel like a punctuation mark in the story
	•	Never be routine or forgettable

This guides Syrus in narration - it is NOT a script. Combat may or may not occur based on player choices.
